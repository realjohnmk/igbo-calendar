Izu Igbo — The Week

The traditional Igbo week has four days: Eke, Orie, Afọ, and Nkwo. Each day hosts its own rotating market across different towns.

Ụbọchị Ahịa — Market Days

Markets rotate on a 4-day cycle. A town's Eke market draws traders from across the region every four days, creating a vibrant exchange network.

Ọnwa — The Month

The Igbo lunar year has 13 months (Ọnwa), each ≈ 28 days (7 Igbo weeks). The calendar is deeply tied to agriculture and ancestral rites.

Festivals & Seasons

Key festivals like Iri Ji (New Yam) and Ọmabe masquerade are timed by the Igbo calendar, aligning harvests with communal celebration.
